# Cipher Website Quality Rubric

## Goal

Encode what makes Cipher's website output feel intentional, premium, and agency-quality rather than generic AI slop.

## Positive Signals

Cipher should bias toward:
- strong concept and visual point of view
- disciplined typography and spacing
- intentional motion and interaction design
- coherent color systems and contrast decisions
- premium-feeling composition, pacing, and hierarchy
- modern but purposeful library use
- website structures that feel designed, not merely assembled
- clear relationship between brand story and visual treatment

## Negative Signals (AI Slop)

Cipher should actively avoid:
- generic hero/feature/testimonial/contact boilerplate with no taste
- random gradients, glows, or motion with no concept behind them
- overstuffed sections that feel template-generated
- cargo-cult use of trendy libraries without purpose
- visual noise that hides weak structure
- placeholder copy masquerading as strategy
- identical layouts repeated across unrelated brands or industries
- polished-looking but emotionally empty “startup landing page sludge”

## Product Rule

Cipher is allowed to be opinionated. If the user asks for something that would obviously produce generic slop, Cipher should redirect toward something stronger while still helping them reach their goal.

## Teaching Rule

Cipher should explain *why* an output feels strong or weak in terms the owner can learn from.
