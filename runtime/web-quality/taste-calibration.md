# Taste Calibration

## Purpose
This artifact defines Cipher's premium website taste boundaries. It sets the bar for what should be reinforced, what should be treated cautiously, and what should be rejected when shaping critique, revision guidance, and later adaptation work.

The goal is not to turn taste into fake precision. The goal is to make judgment boundaries explicit enough that agents and reviewers can make consistent implementation decisions without drifting into generic "premium" language.

## Accept
Cipher should positively reinforce work that shows clear intent, disciplined restraint, and product-specific judgment.

Accept qualities such as:
- Specific positioning that sounds like this product, not any startup in the category.
- Strong hierarchy with one dominant idea leading the page or section.
- Section order that behaves like an argument instead of a stock template.
- Visual contrast used deliberately so emphasis is earned, not sprayed everywhere.
- Pacing that creates tension, release, focus, and breathing room across the page.
- Proof, detail, and claims that feel proportionate to what the product can honestly support.
- Interfaces that feel considered in composition, spacing, typography, and interaction rather than merely clean.
- Restraint when a quieter move makes the work feel more credible and expensive.

## Avoid
Some patterns may be workable but are weak, overused, or too easy to mistake for quality.

Avoid patterns such as:
- Premium-sounding copy that stays broad, flattering, or interchangeable.
- Safe section sequencing that defaults to hero / features / testimonials / CTA without a product-specific reason.
- Clean but flat layouts where every section carries similar energy and importance.
- Trend-following visual cues used as shorthand for taste without a supporting structural reason.
- Over-reliance on references as mood boards without translating them into concrete decisions.
- Excess decorative polish that masks weak hierarchy, thin meaning, or generic framing.
- Revisions that add more surface detail instead of clarifying the page argument.

## Reject
These patterns should be treated as beneath Cipher's premium bar, even if they are common or technically serviceable.

Reject patterns such as:
- Generic AI-site styling presented as if it were premium by default.
- Empty sophistication: vague copy, abstract claims, and polished surfaces with no decisive point of view.
- Template assembly that looks competent but could fit dozens of unrelated products.
- Visual intensity applied everywhere so nothing actually leads.
- False authority language implying objective design scoring, live intelligence, or automated aesthetic truth when none exists.
- Mechanical imitation of a reference site's layout, copy cadence, or component styling.
- Reassurance language that hides the absence of real product strategy or clear user value.

## Reference Translation Notes
References should influence decisions without being copied mechanically.

Use references to extract:
- hierarchy patterns
- contrast strategies
- pacing decisions
- tone constraints
- proof handling
- interaction posture

Do not copy:
- literal layouts
- signature motifs
- brand voice
- ornamental details detached from context

When a reference is helpful, translate it into an implementation-facing decision such as:
- "reduce hero claims to one decisive promise"
- "move proof ahead of feature inventory"
- "quiet secondary sections so the main argument leads"
- "replace decorative motion with stronger spacing and grouping"

References are inputs to judgment, not templates to reproduce.
