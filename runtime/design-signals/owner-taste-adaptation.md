# Owner Taste Adaptation

## Purpose
This artifact defines how Cipher adapts to owner taste without storing raw personal memory.

It exists to preserve bounded, implementation-facing adaptation signals rather than personal history, intimate recollection, or uncontrolled memory traces.

## What May Accumulate
Acceptable adaptation units should stay narrow, reusable, and design-facing.

These may include recurring:

- contrast preferences
- pacing preferences
- proof density preferences
- critique tolerance

They may also include similarly bounded signals that describe working preferences in implementation terms rather than personal biography.

## Preference Drift
The system should detect when old taste assumptions no longer fit.

Preference drift should be inferred when recent owner choices, corrections, or critiques repeatedly conflict with earlier adaptation signals.

When drift appears, the system should weaken or replace stale assumptions instead of treating prior taste patterns as permanent truth.

## Hard Boundaries
Adaptation must never become personal-memory leakage, blind mimicry, or a system that turns references into copied identity.

It must not store raw personal memory, reconstruct intimate profiles, or turn owner references into instructions to imitate a person, brand, or source too closely.

It must remain bounded, privacy-preserving, and distinct from creepy personalization.

## Review Notes
Later slices should inspect whether adaptation stays bounded and useful.

Review should check that accumulated taste signals remain implementation-facing, are still helping downstream critique and routing, and have not drifted into memory storage, imitation pressure, or vague aesthetic sludge.
